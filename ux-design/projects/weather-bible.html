Weather Bible

(iOS, Android)

Weather Bible is a simple single-day app project that allows the user to see the current weather for any city along with a bible verse that relates to that form of weather.

Designing with Whimsy

Context

This app is something that I created and released over the course of 16 hours back in 2017 as a fun, light-hearted project to gain some experience going through the mobile app development process. After 2+ years of learning design and development, in 2020 I completely redesigned and redeveloped the app to match my current understanding of professional design and development principles.

Process

The design of this app was mostly an exercise in user interface design, as the concept does not particularly solve any real need. As such, most of my initial research revolved around analyzing other popular weather apps and how they organize and present information.

For my redesign, I had plenty of learnings and feedback from the initial release to work with, such as giving the user the option of using their GPS location or not (I preferred not to collect location data initially, to allow the user full control of their data-tracking, but some users preferred the inverse) and a better eye for font selection and consistency. I also really enjoy illustration and animation, so the redesign gave me a great opportunity to draw more fun characters and objects by hand as I did for the Foxy Birds remake.

The main features that needed to be present were the real-time weather, a bible verse matched to that form of weather, and animated imagery and characters that changed with the weather. With this in mind, I sketched out the plans for the app including a basic user journey, the MVP features, and a timeline to complete the project.

Outcome

An app idea doesn't always have to be complicated, or even intended to make money. Sometimes we have ideas that are just fun to realize, and we can learn a lot in the process of doing so. The redesign, as well as the initial version of this app were a lot of fun to make, and they were even more enjoyable to see in the hands of my friends and family.

Cohesive Series Design

Context

I got started with the More Human Podcast in June of 2020, as an extension of my year-long email newsletter series on mental and emotional health. One thing I knew I really wanted to do beyond creating the podcast itself was to use each week as an opportunity to make artwork in a cohesive style for each episode’s cover. I have been fascinated with the breadth and charm of mid-century modern graphic design, so I decided to go with a similar approach for the podcast cover and the rest of the episode covers in the first season.

Challenges

I tend to overcomplicate things and sometimes I get caught up in the “more/bigger is always better” ideology, so I used this space to experiment with simplification and visual suggestion of an idea, rather than fully-rendered realism. This is where my discovery of the mid-century modern aesthetic really took hold, and it has been a really fun challenge so far!

Outcome

I’m really content with the episode covers and the consistent color scheme that I’ve used to connect them all. The pattern is clean and recognizable, so that my podcast branding can be recognized anywhere on the internet, whether by the podcast artwork itself, or from any episode in the series.

Designing the Church Online host platform

Context

In the last week of my internship, I worked on the redesign of the host experience of the Church Online platform. I completed the internship before the design could be implemented, but the project gave me valuable UX Design experience in an organization and I loved and learned a lot from the process.

Process

I began the project by exploring the then-current design and interviewing the stakeholders for their perspective and commonly-reported issues with the that design. After consulting with the team's design lead, I started to ideate some possible solutions for the key issues, most notably moving between the various chat panels of hosts, guests, and private conversations during the live sermons.

Challenges

The major challenge was that even though the users had issues with the then-current design, they had grown accustomed to using it anyway, and in the real-time sessions in which they were serving as hosts, they didn't have the time to learn a completely new interface. This led to design choices that fixed the major concerns without completely revamping the look and feel of the interface.

Outcome

I gained valuable experience during this project in terms of communicating with stakeholders, mapping user desires to design decisions, and validating design decisions with solid user-centered reasoning. My internship ended after I passed the design through to the lead designer for approval, so I was not present to see user feedback or oversee implementation issues that might arise, but the journey of designing the experience was surely impactful, both for me and for the end users volunteering their time on the Church Online platform every week.

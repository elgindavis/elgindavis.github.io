My Daily Bread

(iOS, Android)

My Daily Bread is a mobile app created to allow Christians to take notes on
key scriptures as they engage with the Word of God.

Designing the Experience

Context

The initial research for this project came informally over the course of a few years with observations of a potential problem. When I would go to church on Sundays, I noticed people taking notes on the sermon and scriptures in paper journals and notepads.

When the service ended, they would tuck away their notepads, and in most cases, probably not look at the notes again until the next Sunday. While some people take notes simply because it helps them retain information better (whether they ever look at the notes again or not), I saw that there might be an opportunity to improve this experience.

Process

The entire first iteration of the project took a bit less than 3 weeks to complete. I started the design of the app by thinking through all of the different possibilities of actions that a user might take, and what their respective interfaces might look like. The key step here was to differentiate the minimum viable product features from the nice-to-have features.

After narrowing down my scope, I went through a few rounds of paper-wireframing to show the user flow and possible actions that the users may take in navigating the app. I continued to refine this flow until I landed on a flow that appears minimal enough to let the user take full control of their experience.

Later in this stage, I noted the jobs that the user needs to get done in the app and took the process a step further by questioning why each job was important, and then answering why it is important to better understand the user's needs.

Challenges

Some of the biggest challenges in designing this app were figuring out how to teach the user how to use it or, better yet, how to make it unbelievable intuitive so that every action felt obvious and natural for the users.

Usability testing with the prototype and live in-app data collection with Google Analytics have been my main methods of testing the user experience of the app, which were really helpful in working through these challenges. Both helped me gain honest insight into the app's experience, as well as solid steps to improve the experience overall over the past few years.

Outcome

The app ended up having over 2,500 unique and organic installs (no marketing was done for the app) in countries all across the world, including both the iOS and Android platforms. This verified the relevance of the problem and the need for this particular solution. After numerous design and development iterations, the app is finally firing on all cylinders and helping people across the globe take their spiritual journeys to new heights.

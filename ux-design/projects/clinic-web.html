TeleVet B2B Web App

Software Engineer, Accessibility and Usability Leader

A (second) quick-to-market, React-based MVP web app

MVP web app for Veterinarians

Context

In this project, I worked mostly as a software engineer, filling in the design and knowledge gaps where usability and accessibility standards needed improvement before or during implementation. I worked alongside a team of engineers, a designer, and a project manager. Over the course of about two months, we worked to build a quick-to-market B2B minimum viable product web application to serve a major need in the veterinarian community and replace our legacy application with 1,000+ existing veterinary practice clients.
*Visuals are not able to be displayed

Process

  • With respect to the design process, I led the engineering team in shaping the product roadmap with respect to accessibility/usability concerns.
  • I applied accessibility tooling to the in-phase product to ensure our team produced a product that meets universal accessibility standards (using tools such as Google Lighthouse, disability simulators, contrast checkers, etc)
  • I handled major portions of the UI/UX implementation and reviewed incremental design changes for user experience, usability, and accessibility standards, and made notes, led review discussions as necessary.

Challenges

  • Developing code without finished design— One of the biggest challenges was starting development without having finished designs. By using my knowledge of design principles, I was able to implement usable and accessible features where design had not been completed.
  • Being the only team member focused on accessibility— Many product development teams do not think about accessibility until they get complaints from users. I don’t believe it is right to exclude people of any ability level from using any product, so I make sure to be a strong voice for accessibility in every project. For this project, I used my voice and my set of accessibility tools (and proximity to the actual code being written) to ensure that the final product would be usable by any customer.

Outcome

We reached the MVP launch on-time and were able to generate a lot of excitement from our users in our live demos. At the time of writing, we are just getting started in the lifetime of the project.

Foxy Birds

(iOS, Android)

Foxy Birds is an endless-runner, arcade-based mobile game I created independently in 2019.

Designing the Game

Context

Foxy Birds is a personal side project and revamp of my 2018 mobile iOS game Foxy Birds. The game is an endless-runner-style mobile experience that I designed and developed independently during my final semester of university in the fall of 2019. I knew how I wanted the game to work, based on the feedback I had gotten from the players of the original Foxy Birds mobile game over the course of the year, so the implementation in Unity was pretty straightforward.

My main focus in making this game was to design an experience that would transport the player into a vintage, monochromatic, cartoon world that gives the feeling of being a part of an old-time 1920s TV show.

I was really excited to put my learnings from Foxy Birds version 1 to use, as well as my curiosity for different forms of creativity. I challenged myself to tackle every part of the game’s creation— from the hand-drawn artwork to the coding implementation, the animations and background music, as well as the experience design and game idea.

Process

I started my exploration with research into other games made in this style, such as the critically-acclaimed Cuphead and Bendy and the Ink Machine.

I love this vintage style, so I gathered several references from these games identify the foundations of the experience, which I found to be the grungy textures, pie-eyed style of artwork, retro fonts and, of course, the bouncing characters moving to the swingin’ jazz music.

Over the course of two months, I wrote the code in C#, designed the basic character animations, created a single swing jazz background track, designed the gameplay controls, and tied it all together in the Unity3D editor.

Gameplay

Seeing as mobile devices don’t have much real estate to begin with, I opted for an invisible control system to maximize visibility of the player and the moving objects. The target demographic consists of individuals who are accustomed to gaming systems like Xbox or Playstation that usually have a joystick on the left side that controls movement, and buttons on the right that control other aspects of the player’s capabilities.

This design decision made the creation of a clear tutorial absolutely necessary. After user-testing with around ten early users on Android, I concluded that the tutorial was sufficient to onboard the players in my target demographic.

Outcome

The outcome of this project was overwhelmingly positive, and most of the users that I tested the game with noted the uniqueness of the hand-drawn style and the feeling of really being in a 1920s cartoon scene.

This was such a fun project to work on, and the experience I’ve gained from the entire game development process continues to drive my passion for game design and creating unique, fun, and personal interactive experiences.

ABOUT

Originally from Palm Beach County, Florida, Elgin is passionate about changing the world through education and humanitarian efforts. He spent a few weeks in South Africa teaching computer literacy in January 2017 working toward both of these goals, and is currently working to improve the available opportunities for the youth of Mamelodi.

After some major injuries curtailed his college football career, Elgin is focusing his time on learning new languages, traveling and interacting with people of all types, and innovating a better world through education and a general understanding of our fellow human beings.

He spent the summer of 2016 working as a volunteer for Harvard Habitat for Humanity, raising money for HFH's builds throughout community, and he spent the summer of 2017 working with OpenClassrooms, acting as mainly as a Web Developer Intern.

During the internship, Elgin worked within the Tech Team in Quality Assurance doing both manual and automated-code testing, and he also worked within the Education Team reviewing and editing degree-earning path specifications (including one at the Master's degree level), helping to translate the workflow platform for degree-earning-path creation, helping to redefine a new skill-based education assessment platform, and co-writing and co-starring in an upcoming OpenClassrooms course. He enjoys art, drawing, and philosophy.

Future projects interests include: UX/UI Design, Front-End Web Development, and any project for the greater good.

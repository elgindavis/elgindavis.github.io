Results

Overall, we found that for all regions of the US, using PCA with a high number of components will result in a very high R2 value, and we take this as being a more reliable model in terms of being representative of our data. We also found that out of all of the regions, the Southeast consistently has the highest murder rates across all of the predictors, and the West generally has the lowest. Our most significant predictors of murder rate across the 5 regions, from both visual and statistical analysis, were race, employment status, marital status, college degree attainment, income, and median age of females.




Strengths and Shortcoming

We were able to achieve a very high test score using PCA with a high number of components. That we were able to acheive such a score is a strength. However, a shortcoming of our analysis in general is that one-hot encoding our MSA areas gave us a very high number of predictors. This may have falsely increased our R^2 values in general. In addition, we note that there may have been significant patterns associated with time that we were unable to analyze or take advantage of because we have not yet learned about time series.

Further Work

If we'd had more time, our next steps for our project would include analyzing the interactions between what we found to be the most significant predictors, and diving deeper into the political and social aspects that differentiate each of these regions from one another. We could also begin to incorporate the concept of time series to analyze the changes in each city, state, and region over time with respect to our main predictors.

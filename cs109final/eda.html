Exploratory Data Analysis

Brief Overall Findings 

Overall, we found that for all regions of the U.S., using PCA with a high number of components will result in a very high R^2 value. We also found that out of all of the regions, the Southeast consistently has the highest murder rates across all of the predictors, and the West generally has the lowest. Our most significant predictors of murder rate across the 5 regions, from both visual and statistical analysis, were race, employment status, marital status, college degree attainment, income, and median age of females.

Race in America

Here we analyze the significance of race in the analysis of metropolitan murder in the U.S.

PreviousNextClose
We note first of all that the Southeast (SE) region consistently has the highest murder rates across all of the predictors. We see the greatest disparity in the scatter plot showing the breakdown of black populations across these major cities. Not only does the SE have the greatest black population, it also generally correlates with the highest murder rates of all of the regions.
Inversely, we see that cities with lower white populations in the SE seem to correlate with higher murder rates. In these cases, the EDA seems to imply that race, notably the major races, play a major role in the overall murder rates of major cities and their encompassing regions. With respect to the asian populations, the highest murder rates appear in cities with the smallest asian populations. This suggests that asian populations most likely do not have a significant effect on murder rates nationwide.
With respect to population, most of our data points come from cities with populations less than a quarter of a million people, so it is difficult to note any significant correlation between population and murder rate.
We are not able to come to a conclusion from the data visualization alone, but we are given some noteable indications as to what we can expect to see in our future modeling. 

Percent Unemployed

In this plot, the visual relationship between percent unemployed and murder rate is unclear, but the plot as a whole does not seem to follow the general pattern that we see for the insignificant predictors. We observe some type of interaction here, but we are uncertaina how exactly these two seem to correlate, which may become useful in guiding our later model creation. 

Median Household Income

Median household income is one of the biggest predictors that we hypothesized to impact the murder rate, and we can see a noteable correlation in this chart. The cities with the lowest median income correlate with the highest murder rates, again most prominently in the Southeast.

Recent College Grads

In populations with more recent college graduates, we see a correlation with lower murder rates. It is not a linear relationship, so the data suggests that we may have more significant predictors that play a role than the college graduate percentage by itself.

Median Age

Here we analyze the median ages for the entire population, as well as individually for males and females

PreviousNextClose
The median age of ~38 seems to be the most common across the country, so it makes sense that average-aged communities see the highest murder rates. We observe the highest murder rates in the SE, but all of the regions seem to have the highest murder rates around the same median age. One thing that is worth noting is how the extreme median ages- the youngest and the oldest- each have lower murder rates than we see in the middle aged section. The cities with median ages over 50 years seem to have the lowest murder rates, but other than this observation, we do not gain too much additional insight from this visualization.
